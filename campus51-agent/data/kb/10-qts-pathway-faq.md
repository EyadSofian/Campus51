# QTS Pathway & Assessment Route — Frequently Asked Questions

The **Qualified Teacher Status (QTS) Pathway** is one of Campus 51's flagship programs. This FAQ covers eligibility, structure, fees, recognition, and the application process.

---

## What is QTS?

**Qualified Teacher Status (QTS)** is a professional teaching certification that demonstrates an educator has met the required teaching standards. It is widely recognized internationally and supports career progression in international and British curriculum schools.

---

## What is the difference between QTS and an iPGCE?

- **iPGCE** is an *academic* qualification focused on educational theory.
- **QTS** is a *professional teaching status* confirming a teacher has met professional standards through assessment and evidence of classroom practice.

Many educators complete **both** for combined academic + professional credentials.

---

## Does the iPGCE count when applying for QTS?

Yes. Completing an iPGCE can support the QTS journey by strengthening pedagogical knowledge. However, applicants must still meet the QTS assessment requirements and teaching standards.

---

## Eligibility — QTS Preparation Pathway

To join the QTS Preparation Pathway, applicants must meet:

### Academic Qualifications
- Recognised Bachelor's degree (equivalent to a UK Honours degree at 2:2 or above).

### Teaching Experience
- Currently teaching within a school setting.

### English Language
- If English is not the first language: IELTS Academic score of **7.0** (or equivalent).

### School Requirements
- A school recommendation letter confirming teaching role and school support.

---

## Eligibility — Assessment Only (AO) Route

For the Assessment Only Route, applicants need:

### Academic & Qualification Requirements
- GCSE Grade C / Grade 4 (or equivalent) in:
  - English
  - Mathematics
  - Science (Primary applicants only)
- International qualifications accepted subject to equivalency review.

### Teaching Experience
- At least **2 years** of teaching experience.
- Currently working as an unqualified teacher (paid or unpaid).
- Teaching within a suitable school setting.
- Experience teaching across **consecutive age ranges**.
- Teaching experience in **two or more schools/settings**, including the current school.

### School & Mentor Support
- A school declaration form.
- Professional references.
- Access to lesson observations.
- A mentor / school-based assessor.

### Safeguarding & Documentation
- Two professional references.
- Safeguarding / DBS or equivalent background check.
- Health and fitness to teach declaration.
- Identity verification document.

## How long does the QTS pathway take?

The pathway typically takes:
- **28 weeks** of professional learning, followed by
- **12 weeks** of assessment.

**Total: approximately 40 weeks**, depending on progress and readiness.

---

## Is the programme fully online?

Most QTS pathways are delivered **online or blended**:
- Online materials
- Live sessions
- Mentor meetings
- Portfolio submissions
- Virtual observations

Some providers may require occasional in-person components.

---

## Are there scheduled sessions or is it self-paced?

The programme **combines flexible self-paced study with scheduled live sessions, webinars, and tutorials** — designed to provide guidance, support, and professional discussion.

---

## Is there a separate fee for the assessment?

**Yes.** The assessment phase has a separate fee from the QTS pathway tuition. This fee is paid in advance before the assessment stage begins and covers the formal QTS certification assessment process.

---

## How globally recognised is QTS?

QTS is highly respected internationally, particularly in **British curriculum** and **international schools**. Recognition may vary by country — educators are encouraged to check local regulations for their specific region.

---

## What are the benefits of obtaining QTS?

- **Strengthens professional credibility.**
- **Meets international school recruitment requirements.**
- **Opens access to wider teaching opportunities.**
- **Develops reflective practice.**
- **Enhances leadership and career progression.**

---

## What happens after completing the assessment route?

Candidates receive:
- **Official QTS certification.**
- **Teacher Reference Number (TRN)** registered through the **UK Department for Education**.

This supports career progression, leadership opportunities, and international mobility.

---

## Does the assigned mentor need to hold QTS?

**Not necessarily.** The mentor should be:
- An experienced educator familiar with teaching standards.
- Able to observe lessons and provide professional feedback.
- In a supervisory or leadership role within the school.

---

## Are observations conducted physically or recorded?

Observations may be:
- Live in person
- Virtual (live online)
- Recorded teaching sessions submitted by the candidate

The mentor or assessor reviews practice against teaching standards.

---

## What type of evidence is required?

Candidates submit:
- Lesson plans
- Recorded teaching sessions
- Samples of student work
- Assessment evidence
- Reflective journals
- Observation reports
- Professional development records

---

## How are candidates assessed throughout?

Assessment includes:
- Classroom observations
- Professional discussions
- Portfolio reviews
- Evidence against teaching standards
- Reflective practice submissions

---

## Is there a final exam?

**No.** Most QTS assessment routes focus on professional teaching practice and evidence rather than traditional written examinations.

---

## Can educators outside the UK apply for QTS?

**Yes.** Many QTS pathways are designed specifically for international educators teaching outside the UK.

---

## Do I need to teach a British curriculum to apply?

**Not necessarily.** Educators from different curriculum backgrounds may still be eligible as long as they can demonstrate effective teaching practice aligned with professional standards.

---

## What level of English proficiency is required?

Applicants must demonstrate English language proficiency through an **IELTS Academic score of 7.0 (or equivalent)**, particularly if English is not their first language.

---

## Is previous teaching experience required?

**Yes.** Most assessment routes require candidates to already have classroom teaching experience and responsibility for planning, teaching, and assessment.

---

## Can teaching assistant experience count?

Teaching assistant experience **alone may not always meet eligibility**. Candidates are usually expected to have **independent teaching responsibilities**.

---

## What subjects and age groups are accepted?

Most programmes support a range of subjects and key stages, including **primary and secondary education**. Confirm subject-specific requirements with Campus 51 directly.

---

## How do I know if this pathway is suitable for me?

The QTS pathway is suitable for educators who:
- Are currently teaching.
- Want internationally recognised certification.
- Are seeking career progression.
- Want to work in British or international schools.
- Are committed to reflective professional practice.

**Yes.** The QTS pathway is designed to fit around professional commitments — most candidates complete it while working full-time.
